Don't Fall asleep at the wheel!

Why do we need this drowsiness alert system?

According to the Global Road Safety Report 2015, 141526 persons were killed and approximately 500000 people injured in India because of road crashes. The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration reported a total 72000 crashes, 44000 injuries and 800 deaths due to drowsy driving in 2013.

Don't Drink and Drive! Don't Drowse and Drive!

How does this alert system help?

Our drowsiness alert system detects when you become drowsy using real-time video surveillance and plays a alarm to wake you up and suggests a break. It is highly recommended that you take a break from driving as soon as possible and resume only when you are fully rested.

For a safe and happy journey, please ensure you follow these guidelines Basic safety guidelines!

Don't forget your seatbelt!

We cannot emphasize this enough - always wear your seatbelts at all times and ensure all the other passengers do the same.

Focus!

• Keep 100% of your attention on driving at all times.
• Avoid multi-tasking.
• Don’t use your phone or any other electronic device while driving.

Think before you act

The incessant pressure by other drivers in a busy road can be unsettling, so stay cool and make sure you have interpreted the traffic situation correctly. Exercise patience and "hang back" when necessary

Additional guidelines

• Don't try to pick up something that has fallen down when you are at the wheel.
• Have items needed within easy reach – such as toll fees, toll cards and garage passes.
• Drive sober and drug-free.
• Don't allow children to fight or climb around in your car – they should be buckled in their seats at all times.
• Avoid driving when you're tired.
• Be aware that some medications cause drowsiness and make operating a vehicle very dangerous.
• Always use caution when changing lanes.
• Cutting in front of someone, changing lanes too fast or not using your signals may cause an accident or upset other drivers.

Defensive driving

• Be aware of what other drivers around you are doing, and expect the unexpected.
• Assume other motorists will do something crazy, and always be prepared to avoid it.
• Keep a 2-second cushion between you and the car in front of you. Make that 4 seconds if the weather is bad.
• Slow down. Speeding gives you less time to react and increases the severity of an accident.

Don't forget to take *BREAKS* and use *BRAKES* while driving!
